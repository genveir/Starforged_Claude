**Session 11 Log**

**Opening Vignette**

The bridge of the Steady Return is quiet, Graff at the comm station speaking in a low steady voice. Below in the crew lounge three people sit around a table, cards out, cups cold. A fourth comes down the companionway fast: Gerald is on the line. All three are on their feet before the sentence is finished, crowding the base of the stairs, catching fragments of Graff's half of the conversation. Nobody speaks.

---

**Beat 1 — The Ground Picture**

Gerald gives Graff a full report: Bormann dead, the lead surveyor dead, settlers controlled but alive. The occupation has been restrained — no systematic brutality, no further executions. His honest read was that pulling back and letting the raiders finish might be the cleanest option. Graff tells him about the supply ship. It arrived while Gerald was dark, was turned away by the raiders, and is now sheltering in the adjacent system. It won't return until the orbit is clear.

---

**Beat 2 — The Ration Crackdown**

Gerald puts the supply question to Carl on a second channel. Carl is quiet for a moment, then: *"So that's why."* Word came down less than an hour ago — the raiders locked down the food stores, put the settlement on reduced rations, and roughed up the settlers who protested. Several people were dragged to the jail. At reduced rations, Carl puts the clock at three weeks, no more. Gerald relays the figure to Graff.

---

**Beat 3 — Forcing the Issue**

Gerald tells Graff the passive option is gone. They have no way to know how long the raider operation takes, and the settlement doesn't have the runway to find out. They'll have to make staying too costly and push the raiders out, then call the supply ship back once the orbit is clear. Graff asks if Gerald can do it. Gerald tells him yes — he's dealt with gangs before, even if they didn't have warships giving them cover from orbit. Graff asks what he needs.

---

**Beat 4 — The Plan**

Gerald lays out the shuttle fiction: Paul Ortoff takes the shuttle down into the interference layer and sits quiet. From the raider's perspective, another commando insertion. Gerald then blows the cable running to the mines — whatever the raiders are doing there, it stops. No local fingerprints, pressure builds, settlers stay invisible. Graff agrees without deliberation and tells Gerald he'll send Paul. Gerald confirms he will call back when the shuttle should take off again. From the viewpoint of the raider, another successful extraction.

---

**Beat 5 — Back Down**

Gerald stows his weapons in the relay tower maintenance locker and rides back down to the complex with Maria, running it as a routine maintenance return. The gate raider doesn't look twice. Maria knows exactly where the mining explosives are stored and which shifts leave the magazine quietest. *(Face Danger +heart, Maria bonus applied: strong hit. Momentum 4 → 5.)*

**Beat 6 — Just Like I Thought**

Parking the buggy, Maria tells Gerald he's exactly what she thought he would be — that she'd been feeling hopeless until he arrived. Gerald tells her the job was never about one man dashing in and saving everyone; it's about collecting people, making them see they're a team. They're going to make this right together.
