## Session Log

## Session 4

### Opening Vignette
The shuttle sits behind a ridge near the settlement, cold and unpowered. Two raiders on patrol pass within thirty metres of it without seeing it. Gerald had read the patrol patterns before he crossed and was confident about the coverage gaps. He was wrong about this one — the route ran closer to his landing site than he calculated. He doesn't know that yet.

### Davel's Recommendation
Back in the industrial infrastructure, Gerald handed a problem to Davel. He needed someone on the survey arm who could read mining system data and wouldn't fold under pressure. Davel didn't hesitate long. Sandra Vael — one of the four survey team leads, currently in the settlement on rotation. Quiet woman, comes off slow until she says one thing and you realise she was three steps ahead. Whip smart, iron core. Her team came in just before the raiders arrived. Carl knew her by manifest and supply requests and didn't contradict anything Davel said.

The survey arm is watched more closely than the miners. Sandra couldn't be brought to them. Gerald would have to go to her.

### The Crossing
Gerald gathered what the men knew about raider patterns and blind spots — the repair crews moving like ghosts, too many miners for the raiders to have catalogued by face. Useful cover if someone stopped him. Then he went alone, walking like a man with somewhere to be. Two raiders at a junction glanced at him and looked away. He reached the inn without incident.

### Sandra Vael
Sandra was at a corner table, working through something on a personal pad. She didn't know Gerald's face. When he sat down she took him for a raider and said so, with the flat controlled anger of someone who had been managing it for several days. Gerald waited until she was done, offered his hand, and told her plainly who he was and how he'd arrived. She went very still.

She agreed to look at the pad, but on a condition: Minka Brinberg, the lead surveyor and her direct superior, is being permitted to collect personal effects from her cabin tomorrow under escort. Sandra has been watching that window open and close with no one capable of acting on it. She wanted Gerald for the escort ambush. The timing is wrong and the plan has problems. Gerald agreed anyway. Sandra is now in the circle of trust.

### What the Pad Revealed
Sandra read the data from Hamzi's listener carefully and without hurry. When she set the pad down her expression had shifted — pieces clicking into place that she wished hadn't. She told Gerald about the survey readings: spectacular by any measure, subsurface concentrations outside the standard mineral indices, geometric regularities in the deep scan data inconsistent with any natural formation. Her team had flagged it. Minka had known immediately it was significant.

They had thought they'd found something that would make the settlement rich. Looking at the data flowing through Hamzi's connection — the software specialist feeding survey coordinates into whatever device the raiders had carried in — the shape of it became clear. The raiders were never here for the minerals. The minerals were the map. Something is buried under this moon, and the raiders came specifically to find it. Sandra doesn't know what it is. Neither does Gerald. Not yet.

### End State
The circle of trust now numbers five: Gerald, Carl, Davel Oren, Hamzi, and Sandra Vael. The raiders' true purpose is understood in broad terms. The immediate pressure is Sandra's condition — Minka Brinberg's escort tomorrow, a bad plan on a tight clock. Seventeen days on the supply clock, possibly less.
