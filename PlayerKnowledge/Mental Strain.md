# Narrative Mental Strain Reference — Gerald Finch

## Character Psychological Profile

Gerald Finch is a highly experienced frontier marshal with approximately twenty years of operational history. He is characterised by high empathy, high intelligence, and a hard operational capability that coexists with genuine emotional sensitivity. He is an effective people manager who reads others accurately and invests in them. He has a clear internal distinction between functional suppression (putting things in the box) and genuine emotional processing, and he is aware of this distinction in himself. This self-awareness is a therapeutic asset — he is likely to engage genuinely with psychological support frameworks rather than resist or perform compliance.

His career has required him to build a working model of human psychology in order to function — reading frightened communities, managing trauma in others, making judgments about people under extreme stress. This makes therapeutic frameworks legible and non-threatening to him. He does not need to be convinced that psychological processing is legitimate or useful.

## Session 15 — Pre-existing State at Campaign Entry

By the opening of session 15, Gerald has accumulated significant physical and psychological load:
- Multiple physical injuries: bruised left side (hip to shoulder), damaged foot, earlier accumulated wear
- He is operating with a circle of people he has built relationships with and feels responsible for
- The campaign has required him to put a series of difficult things in the box: the cabin ambush (his call, badly executed), Sandra shooting Minka, putting Sandra into a firefight, killing two raiders on the surface, the general weight of command under conditions of constant danger and resource scarcity

He is functional, experienced, and self-managing. The box is not empty.

## Session 15 — Combat and Psychological Events

### The Fight with Aran (Beats 1–8, Session 15; Beats 1–8, Session 16)

Gerald engaged Aran in a prolonged close-quarters fight in the ore processing plant, assisted by Sandra. Key psychological dimensions:

- Gerald was wounded (knife wound to chest, shoulder clipped by Sandra's round, bruised ribs throughout)
- Sandra shot Aran at point blank range on Gerald's call — "Now" — and the round clipped Gerald on the way through
- Gerald went down immediately after the fight ended: legs gave out, lost consciousness, Sandra caught him
- This was a full psychological and physical collapse at the moment of victory.

### Session 16 — Recovery

- Gerald was carried to Sandra's hidden compartment, treated by Inara (the settlement doctor)
- Inara: competent, relentlessly talkative, warm underneath the efficiency, husband in the raider jail. She becomes a recurring figure.
- Gerald spent approximately two days largely immobile, regaining function incrementally
- During recovery he received the circle's intel about the broader situation (Ferris dead, miners armed, backup dish cut, curfew tightened)
- He was unable to act on any of it. The forced passivity during an active crisis was its own psychological strain.

### Session 17, Beat 5 — Foreman's Fatigue / CSR Precursor

Gerald broke down on the comms line during a planning session. The weight of command responsibility became temporarily unbearable. He said "I can't. It's too much. I can't." Davel held him physically. Sandra named it to the circle as "Foreman's Fatigue" — a culturally recognised term in the mining community for a foreman who has made all the right calls but cannot manage all the consequences. The circle responded without crisis: Carl, Sandra, Hamzi, and Maria stepped up and continued planning around him. This was the first full emotional system breakthrough. Spirit recovered from 2 to 5 (Sojourn, Hearten).

### Session 17, Beat 7 — Return to Function

After further rest and a second Sojourn (Heal: health 2 → 4), Gerald put his suit back on, got to his feet, and said "Let's go." This was a chosen return to function, not a declaration of being fine. The distinction is maintained throughout.

## Opening Vignette, Session 18 (Player-Written)

Gerald and Davel on the service track, approaching the Mont, on the way to lead the assault on the missile position and the inn. Gerald self-reported as "flat — no emotions, good or bad, just focused on the mission." Davel named this accurately: "Flat is your head protecting itself. Doesn't mean you're not functional. Just don't mistake functional for fine." Gerald acknowledged he could do tactics but was uncertain about people. Davel proposed the division of labour: Gerald does tactics, Bran (the miners' de facto leader) translates to his crew. Gerald accepted. He asked Davel to stay near. Davel said he wasn't going anywhere.

**Neurological note**: The emotional blunting at this stage is a protective post-crisis flatline state. Executive function intact. Affective responsiveness narrowed. This state is contingent on forward momentum and cognitive control — it runs well when there is a problem to solve and poorly when confronted with helplessness plus catastrophic surprise.

## Session 19 — Anticipated Psychological Events

### The Gunship Moment (During the Compound Action)

The plan called for the gunship to be destroyed on the pad. It will take off instead, causing serious casualties among Gerald's irregular militia. Gerald will be on the other side of the compound on a commando mission and unable to respond. The specific psychological violence of this moment is the simultaneity: he must continue executing the mission in front of him while hearing the casualties happen and being unable to help. This is not the casualties themselves that break the protective state — it is the helplessness combined with catastrophic surprise.

**Anticipated presentation**: Sudden cessation — Gerald stops mid-movement or mid-sentence and goes somewhere internal. Not breakdown in the conventional sense. The stress system converts the flat affect into something that looks like ice-cold calm but is the emotional processing being shunted completely offline. Davel will see it before Gerald does.

**Davel's immediate intervention**: Physical contact (a hand on the arm, present rather than gentle) and a single concrete task-based demand that gives Gerald's executive function something to grip. Then a brief statement — not a question — that functions as permission to defer: naming that he heard it too, that they will account for it after. The word "account" matters: deferred is survivable, abandoned is not. Gerald comes back online. Narrower than before. Running from behind a hard wall rather than the soft protective layer.

### Post-Mission — The Vacuum

After the compound is clear and the enemy warship leaves (calculating correctly that their mission has failed and that bombing civilians and losing a ship have no value), Gerald will have nothing left to plan, execute, or fight. The mission structure that has been sustaining him disappears all at once.

**Anticipated presentation**:
1. A period of disorienting alertness — the nervous system still threat-scanning an environment with no threats, still looking for the next problem. May manifest as low-level agitation or compulsive small tasks.
2. Biological crash — the system that has been running at emergency capacity for days stops. Everything deferred arrives: injuries, sleep debt, the casualties he couldn't prevent, every decision that cost someone something.
3. Emotional flatness that may feel frightening — victory does not feel like anything. The absence of threat is not relief, it is absence. For Gerald, already flat for two days, this could read as the emotional system not coming back. It will, but not immediately.
4. The box arrives — not as a flood but as a sudden weight. Everything in it, at once.

**Gerald's specific vulnerability here**: The gunship casualties are likely the hardest single item. Not because they are the largest cost, but because they happened while he was on the other side of a wall and couldn't help. High-empathy commanders carry this type of loss longest — they can imagine it from every angle, and they know exactly what those moments were like for the people on the ground.

**Davel's role in the vacuum**: He has seen this before. He does not try to make Gerald feel the victory. He does not offer congratulations or debrief. He provides the most basic physical things — sit down, eat something, here, I'm staying. The presence is the intervention. He is waiting for the small specific thing that cracks it open: not the big things, which Gerald will hold, but something sideways and particular — a detail that carries the full weight without announcing itself.

## iCOVER Protocol — Davel's Approach

Davel will run a version of the iCOVER framework (Identify, Come to present, Observe, Validate, Encourage, Resource) adapted to his relationship with Gerald and the specific situation. Key considerations:

**Gerald will not resist the framework**. He is experienced enough to recognise what Davel is doing, and self-aware enough to know he needs it. He may name it quietly, matter-of-factly. Davel's response: "I know you do. Keep going anyway." Gerald's engagement is genuine and chosen, not passive.

**What Gerald may navigate around**: He can be fully present in the protocol and honest at the surface layer while keeping one thing slightly out of reach. Based on the campaign history, the gunship casualties are the most likely candidate — not because he won't go there, but because a man this self-aware also knows exactly how to be thorough without touching the core. Davel will notice the navigation.

**The "I" step**: Davel names what's happening without clinical language, in register with how they already talk to each other. Not a diagnosis. More like the service track vignette — a direct, quiet observation that doesn't leave room for deflection.

**The "C" step**: The most physically grounded step. Immediate sensory anchoring. Davel has demonstrated he can do this without making it feel procedural.

**The "V" step**: Where the dramatic weight lives. Davel must name what Gerald is carrying — the responsibility, the failure of the plan at a critical moment, the specific horror of having been unreachable while people died — and not flinch from it. His credibility from the last two days earns him the standing to do this. Gerald will accept it from him.

**The "E" and "R" steps**: Point back toward function, not fine. Davel can use the service track framing Gerald set himself: you said you could do functional. The bar is still the same bar.

## Broader Psychological Arc Note

Gerald is experienced enough that he has a map of his own psychological processes and a body of evidence that he survives this. He is not frightened by the crash. What his experience also gives him is efficient avoidance patterns — he knows the shape of the process well enough to stay just busy enough, just functional enough, to defer indefinitely. He can manage himself around the contents of the box rather than through them. He has probably done this before, successfully enough that it became a habit.

Davel may be the first person in a while who does not let him just be good at carrying it. The distinction between functional and processed remains the central question of this narrative line. Gerald will be fine in every measurable sense within a reasonable timeframe. Whether he is actually done with what this operation cost him is a different question, and one the narrative may choose to leave open.
