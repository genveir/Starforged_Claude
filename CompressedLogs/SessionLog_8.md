**SESSION 8**

**Opening Vignette**

Hamzi reaches Sandra on the comms while she sits alone in the hidden compartment. He doesn't ask her to explain. He listens, says what needs to be said, and stays on the line until she tells him she's okay — not meaning it entirely, but meaning it enough. An hour later Davel folds himself through the gap and sits across from her in silence. He brings two ration packs. They eat without talking. When he leaves her hands are steady.

**Beat 1 — The Buggy**

Gerald crests a low ridge and finds an abandoned mining survey buggy listing hard into a gully, right front axle snapped clean, engine bay destroyed by the impact. The solar array is still deployed and intact, tracking the sun with patient indifference. The charge indicator on the powercell housing reads a number it should never reach.

**Beat 2 — The Explosion**

Gerald doesn't clock the danger in time. The powercell goes while he is close. In Raiman's Folly's negligible gravity the blast picks him up and keeps going — the buggy dropping away below him, the horizon briefly visible before he crests his arc and begins the long slow return. He gets his feet under him before he lands hard. His left side from shoulder to hip will ache for days. The buggy burns with the clean ferocity of a cell that has finally found somewhere for all that energy to go. Health 4.

**Beat 3 — The Scavenge**

Gerald sweeps the open frame quickly. A spare atmosphere canister, a thermal blanket, two sealed ration packs. He takes everything worth taking and watches the powercell burn itself out. Then he starts walking. Supply 5.

**Beat 4 — The Camp**

Nightfall comes without warning or transition. Gerald finds a position between two rock shelves, inflates his pressure tent, and gets the suit off for the first time all day. His water sensor has been reading false — a fault he catches on the diagnostic cycle, fixed by a software reset that does nothing for the actual reserves. He has enough water. Not comfortably enough. He eats, wraps himself in the thermal blanket, and is asleep before he knows it. Supply 3.

**Interlude**

The sun rises on Raiman's Folly. A new addition sits on top of the hub — a long range laser air-defence cannon, pulled down from the raider's ship and bolted to the settlement's highest point sometime in the night. Inside, behind a porthole on the eastern face, Carl Inswich draws a neat line through yesterday's date in his paper ledger. Counts forward. Counts again. The number doesn't change. Gerald has been off-grid since the cabin. The supply clock does not care.

**Beat 5 — The Crater Rim**

Gerald crests the crater rim as the sun climbs toward its high point. Below him the mining complex fills the crater floor — processing structures, equipment yards, shaft heads. Peaceful from up here. A single suited figure moves between structures far below. Gerald finds a position behind a rock shelf and takes a long look. He is close.

**Beat 6 — The Relay Tower**

A comms relay tower sits two hundred metres along the ridge. Gerald works his way over and hails Davel. Davel tells him to sit tight — two raider on the complex, don't know the schedules well enough to question a buggy heading out. A driver will come up the service track. Twenty minutes, maybe thirty. Gerald asks about Sandra. *Stable,* Davel says. *She'll want to talk to you.* Gerald settles in behind the rock shelf to wait.

**Beat 7 — Arrival**

The buggy comes up the service track on time. A young miner who looks at the rifle once and asks no questions. Twenty minutes of switchbacks down the crater wall, the complex growing larger and more detailed. Gerald steps through a side airlock into a warm maintenance corridor loud with the sound of infrastructure, and arrives.

**Beat 8 — Maria**

The miner waiting for him is young — mid-twenties, Davel's contact. She introduces herself as Maria and wastes no time: two raiders, keeping lazy oversight, never checking these storages. Gerald has time. She wants to know what he's here to do. She is watching him with the intensity of someone prepared to be disappointed.

**Beat 9 — The Lawman**

Gerald pulls off his helmet and grins. *Well damn. Hey Maria. Good to see you got out of Spinfeld.* The wariness drops off her immediately — she knows him. The Lawman. The man you called when things got bad enough. She grew up in Spinfeld, one of the countless villages under Gerald's jurisdiction in his lawman days. Davel told her the name, but she didn't recognize it. He was always just The Lawman. She pulls up a crate, sits down, and tells him what she knows.

**End State**

Gerald is inside the mining complex, helmet off, water in hand, with an ally who has been waiting for exactly this conversation. The complex has sixty miners, two raiders keeping oversight, compromised leadership cooperating with the raiders, and a cable running live to the device on the other side. Supply is critically low. Sandra is stable in the hidden compartment. The circle knows Gerald has arrived through Davel, but has not talked to him yet.
