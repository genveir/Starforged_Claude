# Session Log

## Session 2

### Departure
Gerald departed the Steady Return in the shuttle from the shuttle bay. Graff said little. The shuttle was pushed out unpowered and Gerald freefell toward the moon for the first ten kilometres, running cold and silent, relying on the shuttle's minimal heat signature and the moon's metallicity interference layer for cover.

### The Descent
During the freefall Gerald realised he had miscalculated his arc slightly. The raider's orbital position was cresting the horizon faster than expected. With seconds to spare he executed an improvised radial burn — pitching the nose toward the surface and accelerating hard, then flipping and decelerating before impact. The manoeuvre worked but burned fuel badly. Supply dropped from 5 to 4.

### Surface Approach
Gerald flew low across the rugged terrain toward the settlement outskirts — quiet, careful work, threading the metallicity interference layer and staying below the raider's sensor horizon. He brought the shuttle down behind a ridge approximately one kilometre short of the settlement and continued on foot.

### Observation
From the ridge Gerald spent considerable time observing the settlement through binoculars. He identified the raiders' patrol patterns and the gaps between them, located a maintenance access route into the ore processing infrastructure that avoided the hub where the raiders are concentrated, and spotted a figure near an outlying equipment shelter whose walk he recognised.

### Entry
Gerald crossed the open ground between the ridge and the settlement outskirts during a patrol gap and reached the ore processing shed without incident. He found an unlocked maintenance hatch — nobody locks the crawlway into the ore separator — and slipped inside. His comms crackled to life, but he was unable to reach Steady Return.

### The Foreman
Inside the ore processing shed Gerald came face to face with one of the mining foremen — a man he knows by face from two years of regular stops, not a genuine friend but a familiar one. The foreman was terrified. Gerald steadied him and made the case that helping was the better bet for both of them. The foreman agreed, with complications: on the way to Carl Inswich he told Gerald that the raiders have been visibly excited about something in recent days. They have taken the settlement's software specialist to the jail and ordered Hamzi to establish a connection between the specialist and the main mining systems. Whatever the raiders are looking for, they are now actively working to extract it.

### Arrival
The foreman led Gerald through maintenance passages and equipment bays to Carl Inswich and then slipped away. Gerald opened the door to find Carl looking up from whatever he had been doing — the expression of a man braced for the worst shifting slowly into recognition. Inswich looks older than Gerald remembers. It has not been long since the last visit.

The session ended with Gerald and Carl face to face, the conversation not yet begun.
